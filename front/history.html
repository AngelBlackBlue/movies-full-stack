A Brief History of Cinema


Cinema, or motion picture, is the art of moving images. It began in the late 19th century and has since evolved into one of the most popular forms of entertainment and art globally.


The Beginning (1890s)

The Lumi√®re brothers are often credited with the birth of cinema. In 1895, they held the first public screening of projected motion pictures in Paris, wowing audiences with simple scenes like workers leaving a factory.


The Silent Era

For the first few decades, films were silent. Storytelling relied heavily on visual acting, intercards, and live musical accompaniment. Legends like Charlie Chaplin and Buster Keaton utilized this medium to create timeless comedies.


The Golden Age

The introduction of synchronized sound in the late 1920s (the "talkies") revolutionized the industry. This era saw the rise of the Hollywood studio system and the production of classic films like Gone with the Wind and Citizen Kane.
